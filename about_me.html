About Me

I am a software engineer who is always striving to improve my technical knowledge. Growing up in the 2000s, technology has always been a part of my life, from playing Gamecube games with my sister as a child, to learning to write my own code, my understanding of technology has advanced dramatically over the course of my life.

My computer science journey started in high school, where my high school had a program with the local community college to allow students to graduate from high school and the community college with an associates degree in computer science at the same time. Actually, I technically graduated from the community college before high school. During this time, I gained a foundational knowledge of Java and C++, as well as an understanding of basic coding concepts.

After graduating from high school, I went to Illinois State University, where my knowledge of computers and coding grew even further. Beyond expanding my knowledge of Java and C++, I also learned Python, my favorite programming language. My education extended beyond just the classroom. I also joined the game development club at ISU, where I learned how to use Unity to create video games with a team of fellow students. I also worked as a technical stagehand at ISU, someone who sets up audio and video for events happening throughout campus. I helped set up for everything from concerts to formal dinners and galas. While not directly related to computer science, this did give me experience with a lot of equipment that I previously had not had experience with, as well as experience working with clients setting up events to meet their standards.

After graduating, I was unable to get a job. Since then I have been trying to expand my knowledge further. I built a computer so that I could have a better understanding of the hardware that makes a computer, and also so that I could have a powerful computer I could use to make future projects.
